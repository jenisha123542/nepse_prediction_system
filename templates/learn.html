NEPSE Glossary
DEMAT Account
A dematerialized account that holds your shares electronically through CDSC Nepal. Required to buy and sell stocks on NEPSE.
MeroShare
CDSC's online portal where investors apply for IPOs, check allotment results, and view their DEMAT holdings.
ASBA
Application Supported by Blocked Amount. Your bank blocks the IPO application amount in your account — it's only debited if you receive an allotment.
LTP
Last Traded Price — the most recent price at which a stock was bought or sold during the trading session.
Circuit Breaker
A rule that automatically halts trading in a stock or the whole market if the price moves beyond a set limit (e.g., 5% or 10%).
Floorsheet
A daily transaction record published by NEPSE showing every trade executed, including buyer and seller broker codes.
Rights Share
Additional shares offered to existing shareholders at a discounted price, usually to raise capital. Shareholders can subscribe or sell their rights entitlement.
Bonus Share
Free shares issued to existing shareholders from a company's reserve funds, proportional to their current holdings. Does not change total portfolio value immediately.
Frequently Asked Questions
How do I start investing in NEPSE as a beginner? ▼
Start by opening a DEMAT account through CDSC Nepal via a registered broker. Then register on MeroShare. You can begin by applying for IPOs, which are lower risk, before buying secondary market shares. Always start with blue-chip stocks like NABIL or NICA before exploring smaller companies.
What are NEPSE trading hours? ▼
NEPSE trading hours are Sunday to Thursday, 11:00 AM to 3:00 PM Nepal Standard Time. Pre-open session runs from 10:45 to 11:00 AM. Markets are closed on Fridays, Saturdays, and public holidays.
What is the minimum amount needed to invest in NEPSE? ▼
For IPOs, the minimum application is 10 units at NPR 100 each = NPR 1,000. For secondary market purchases, it depends on the stock price — you can buy as little as 1 unit of any stock. Cheaper stocks like UPPER (NPR 224) allow entry with very small capital.
How does dividend work in Nepal? ▼
Nepali companies typically announce dividends after their AGM. Cash dividends are directly deposited to your bank account linked to your DEMAT. Bonus shares are credited to your DEMAT account. You must hold shares before the book closure date to be eligible.
Is capital gains tax applicable on NEPSE shares? ▼
Yes. As of current Nepal tax law, a 5% capital gains tax applies for individual investors on profits from share sales, and 10% for institutional investors. Losses cannot be carried forward. Dividend income is taxed at 5% at source (TDS).
